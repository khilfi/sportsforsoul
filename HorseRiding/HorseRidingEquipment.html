Equipments

It is no exaggeration to say that equestrianism is an expensive sport, even just in terms of the amount of equipment horseback riders can accumulate over the years. If you are a beginner, though, thereâ€™s no need to be daunted by all of the equestrian products on the market.

Helmet

either you are a beginner or an expert horse rider, wearing helmet is one of the most fundamental safety equipment that you have to wear to protect the head from any possible risks in case of accident.

Boots

a riding boot with a small heel will help prevent your foot slipping through the stirrup and provide some protection if your toes get trampled. Tall boots also protect your leg from chaffing while you ride.

Stirrups

there are many types of stirrups with features to help you stay comfortable and prevent your foot from sliding through if you take a fall. If your foot runs through the stirrup when you fall, you could be dragged and badly hurt.

Safety Vest

help protect your torso if you fall. Crash or safety vests help prevent injury to your internal organs, your spine and your ribs.

Gloves

protect your hands and give you a bit extra strength and grip. This may be useful if your horse pulls. Horses can blister hands by pulling while riding out on the trail.
