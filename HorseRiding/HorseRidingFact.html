Facts

Horseback-riding sports have been popular since ancient times. Today there are many kinds of horseback-riding events, including horse racing, show jumping, and dressage. In horse racing, horses are ridden by riders known as jockeys. They run for a set distance, often with obstacles for them to jump over. In show jumping, riders steer their horses around a course of obstacles, which they have to jump over without making mistakes. In dressage, riders guide their horses to perform a series of exact movements, such as trots, turns, and diagonal steps. Dressage is sometimes called “horse ballet.”

A male horse is called a stallion.

Estimates suggest that there are around 60 million horses in the world.

Because horse’s eyes are on the side of their head they are capable of seeing nearly 360 degrees at one time

Horses are herbivores (plant eaters).

Horses have bigger eyes than any other mammal that lives on land.

Horses have been domesticated for over 5000 years.

Horses have around 205 bones in their skeleton.

A 19th century horse named ‘Old Billy’ is said to have lived 62 years.

Domestic horses have a lifespan of around 25 years.

Horses can run shortly after birth.

A female horse is called a mare.

A young female horse is called a filly.

A young male horse is called a colt.
