History

Equestrianism a general term describes Horse riding or Horseback riding which is refers to the skill and sport of riding, driving or vaulting with horses. This broad description includes the use of horses for practical working purposes, transportation, recreational activities, artistic or cultural exercises, and competitive sport.

As the history tells, the humankind started using a horse as far back as 6000 BC. However, it is believed that horseback riding may have begun around 4500 BC.

There are many controversies regarding the identity of the first people to ride horses. The Brahmins of India claim to be the first horse riders. According to the history of Chinese culture, they started riding horses as early as 4000 BC. However, the earliest record of horse riding found in a tomb in Egypt is believed to date to around 1600 BC. In those times, Egyptians mostly used chariot horses for warfare purposes, besides transportation.

During the Medieval Period from 5th to 15th century, horses were categorized by their usage, the chargers were used for warfare, and pulling carts. The ancient culture of horseback riding has contributed a lot to the current styles of horse riding, namely, the Western and the English riding styles. The basic elements of both styles are very similar to each other; only the purpose and equipment are different.

Nowadays, one can opt for horse racing for recreation, exercise, or even a sports career. Apart from being a great exercise, riding a horse on its bare back is a useful means to learn how to maintain proper balance.
